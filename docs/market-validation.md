# Market Validation

## Summary

The market need is validated, but the MVP should not claim to cover every product-team need.

The best wedge is the knowledge/memory/discovery/docs layer: capture messy product inputs, preserve source-linked evidence, maintain decisions, and propose living-doc updates.

## Signals from existing tools

Established products validate adjacent needs:

- Productboard: customer needs, feedback synthesis, AI for PMs, specs, competitive research.
- Jira Product Discovery: ideas, insights, prioritization, roadmaps.
- Aha!: strategy, discovery, ideas, agile delivery, centralized product knowledge, AI assistant.
- Dovetail: customer intelligence, AI analysis, chat/search over customer data, AI docs/agents.
- Zeda.io: voice-of-customer AI product management, customer signals, opportunities, roadmaps.
- airfocus: AI with product context, triaging feedback, co-authoring PRDs/specs, stakeholder loops.
- Savio: centralize feedback, prioritize feature requests.
- Sprig/Maze: research, surveys, prototype/user testing, research-grade AI.
- Linear: product development, agent-era planning, turning conversations/feedback into issues.
- Notion AI/Confluence AI/Rovo: AI over workspace docs and organizational knowledge.

## Needs coverage

Strong MVP fit:

- discovery capture;
- qualitative feedback synthesis;
- living PRD/spec proposals;
- decision logs and rationale;
- weekly product/team briefs;
- source-linked product context;
- product docs hygiene.

Partial fit later:

- roadmap prioritization;
- issue/backlog hygiene;
- stakeholder loops;
- competitive/research monitoring;
- linking feedback to initiatives in external tools.

Weak fit unless integrated:

- revenue-weighted prioritization;
- product analytics;
- research recruiting/panels;
- surveys/prototype testing;
- full Jira/Linear/Productboard replacement;
- CRM/customer-success workflows.

## Open-source landscape

Relevant similar projects found:

- `lenar-amirov/product-pipeline-public`: Claude Code product discovery pipeline with persistent state, living PRD, evidence-typed hypotheses, and decision log.
- `inanctelci/prd-updater-skill`: Claude skill for continuously updating a living PRD.
- `tristanjkaiser/pm-granola-agent`: Granola meeting-note processor for PM action items and tickets.
- `ben-ripley/ralph-loop`: AI development loop that reads/updates PRD state.

Interpretation: open-source validation exists, but the space is early and fragmented. Hermes can differentiate through multi-platform gateway, profiles, cron, permissions, local/private deployment, and product-team-specific workflows.

## Positioning recommendation

Avoid: "AI product manager."

Use: "Product team memory agent for discovery, decisions, and living docs."
