# User Interview Notes

Source: Product discovery interview notes from a startup PM
Date: 2026-06-10
Interviewee: Jordan Lee
Role: Head of Customer Success at a 45-person B2B SaaS company
Segment: Mid-market customer-facing team lead
Interviewer: PM

## Interview context

Jordan's team collects customer feedback from support tickets, Slack community posts, Gong call snippets, and ad hoc notes from customer success managers. The product team asks for evidence when prioritizing roadmap changes, but the evidence often arrives late or is scattered across tools.

## Current workflow

- Customer success managers paste notable customer feedback into a shared Slack channel.
- Support tickets are tagged inconsistently by issue area.
- PMs manually scan Slack and support before roadmap planning.
- Decision rationale is usually captured in a meeting doc, but source evidence is not copied forward.
- Weekly product updates summarize shipped work better than discovery signals.

## Direct quotes

- "I know we heard this from at least five customers, but I can't prove it quickly when product asks."
- "By the time I collect the links, the planning conversation has usually moved on."
- "Our PRD says onboarding is mostly self-serve, but enterprise customers still need handholding for workspace setup."
- "I don't want another roadmap tool. I want something that remembers what customers said and where it came from."
- "If AI updates the PRD directly, people will stop trusting it. I need proposed edits with sources."

## Pain points

- Evidence is scattered across support, Slack, calls, and PM notes.
- Repeated customer pain is hard to quantify without manual link collection.
- PRD assumptions drift away from recent customer evidence.
- Weekly updates miss discovery signals and open product questions.
- The team worries that AI-generated docs could silently change source-of-truth language.

## Goals

- Keep source-linked customer evidence close to product decisions.
- Turn messy notes into discovery summaries quickly.
- Identify PRD implications without editing the PRD automatically.
- Produce a weekly product brief that highlights discovery signals, decisions, open questions, and recommended next actions.

## Assumptions to validate

- Customer success teams would contribute messy evidence if the capture workflow is lightweight.
- PMs trust AI-generated discovery notes when direct quotes and source links are visible.
- Proposed PRD edits are safer than automatic source-of-truth edits.

## Follow-up questions

- Which source has the highest-value feedback today: Slack, support tickets, calls, or PM notes?
- How many repeated customer mentions should trigger a PRD update proposal?
- Who should approve proposed PRD/spec edits?
- What format makes weekly discovery signals useful to product/design/engineering leads?

## PRD implications

- Reinforces the MVP requirement to preserve source-linked evidence.
- Supports PRD update proposals rather than silent edits.
- Adds weight to weekly product briefs as a core output.
- Suggests support-ticket clustering should be the next validated input type.
