# Product Brainstorm

Source: Simulated founder + PM brainstorm notes
Date: 2026-06-13

Participants: Founder, PM

## Raw idea cluster

- Give founders a single weekly AI-generated customer recap that merges support, calls, and Slack feedback into one brief.
- Let teams click from the recap into source-linked evidence and proposed PRD changes.
- Maybe add a lightweight “confidence meter” so rough themes do not look validated.

## Assumptions

- Founders will reuse a structured customer recap every week if it saves 30+ minutes.
- The highest-value first output is a recap rather than a full research repository.
- Teams will trust generated synthesis more when every claim links back to evidence.

## Hypotheses

- A source-linked weekly recap could become the sticky wedge before deeper product-memory workflows.
- Confidence labeling may reduce fear that brainstorm ideas are being treated as roadmap commitments.
- A proposal-first PRD workflow could make recap outputs feel safe enough to share with engineering.

## Non-goals

- Do not turn this into a full roadmap planning tool.
- Do not auto-prioritize ideas or rewrite `PRD.md` without review.
- Do not build survey distribution or recruiting workflows.

## Risks

- The recap could feel generic if the evidence is too thin.
- Founders may want immediate action recommendations even when confidence is low.
- Too many generated artifacts could create review fatigue.

## Next actions

- Test whether founders actually revisit a weekly customer recap artifact.
- Validate what evidence threshold is needed before showing a PRD proposal.
- Compare recap-only output versus recap plus open questions for usefulness.

## Direct quotes

- "I want one place to glance at the week’s customer signal without losing the original quotes."
- "If it starts acting like a roadmap tool, I’m out."
- "I’d trust it more if it showed me the evidence and called out what is still a hypothesis."
